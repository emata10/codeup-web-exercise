FAQ about national parks

Q1: What national park is the biggest?
A: The biggest national park in the United States is Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve in Alaska.
Q2: What is the oldest national park?
A: The oldest national park is Yellowstone National Park, established in 1872.
Q3: What is the hottest national park?
A: The hottest national park is Death Valley National Park in California, USA.
Q4: What national park has the deepest lake?
A: Crater Lake National Park in Oregon, USA has the deepest lake in the United States.
Q5: What is the smallest national park?
A: Hot Springs National Park in Arkansas, USA is the smallest national park by area.
Q6: Can I bring my pet to a national park?
A: It depends on the national park. Some parks allow pets, while others do not. You should always check the park's website before visiting.
Q7: Which national park has the highest peak?
A: Denali National Park in Alaska, USA has the highest peak in North America, Mount McKinvey.
Q8: What is the most-visited national park?
A: The Great Smoky Mountains National Park is the most-visited national park in the United States.
Q9: What national park has the most geysers?
A: Yellowstone National Park has more than 500 active geysers, which is more than half of all the world's geysers.
Q10: Can I fly a drone in a national park?
A: It depends on the national park. Some parks allow drones, while others do not. You should always check the park's website before visiting.

National Parks

  • Yellowstone National Park

    • Established in 1872
    • Covers over 2 million acres
    • Home to more than 10,000 thermal features
    • Contains the largest concentration of mammals in the lower 48 states

  • Yosemite National Park

    • Established in 1890
    • Covers over 760,000 acres
    • Contains more than 3,000 lakes and ponds
    • Features the tallest waterfall in North America

  • Grand Canyon National Park

    • Established in 1919
    • Covers over 1.2 million acres
    • Features a 277-mile long canyon
    • Contains over 1,500 plant, 355 bird, 89 mammal, and 47 reptile
